Book Description

The cosmos operates according to laws that have no purpose, the advent of life is an accident with no significance, and the universe moves toward randomness, according to the dominant scientific viewpoint. Modern discoveries at the interface of physics, biology, neurology, and information theory, collectively known as "complexity science," are, however, challenging this depressing view of nature. A new cosmic story is taking shape as a result of a better understanding of evolution and recent developments in our knowledge of the phenomena known as emergence: Nature's smallest "parts" combine to build ever-larger "wholes" in an unstoppable process.
